# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MF_DataCenter is used by platform operators, content operators, merchant operators, and AI operations owners. They work inside a back-office context where the main jobs are monitoring business health, confirming metric definitions, spotting missing or abnormal data, reviewing AI knowledge gaps, and turning raw MF_EP data into reusable data assets.

## Product Purpose

MF_DataCenter is an enterprise data-center console for the MF ecosystem. It consolidates marketplace, content, merchant, AI consultation, and future website behavior data into governed metrics, snapshots, quality checks, and reusable APIs. Success means operators can trust what they see, understand where each metric came from, know whether it is fresh, and act without modifying MF_EP business records.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, accountable. The interface should feel like a professional operations tool: quiet enough for repeated use, clear enough for quick diagnosis, and explicit about data lineage and quality.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style dashboards, oversized hero sections, decorative gradients, glassmorphism, novelty controls, dense color decoration, and generic SaaS card walls. Avoid hiding metric definitions in code-only behavior. Avoid data displays that look polished but cannot explain freshness, source, owner, or calculation.

## Design Principles

- Make provenance visible: every important metric should expose source, formula, freshness, and ownership.
- Prefer operating clarity over spectacle: dense information is acceptable when hierarchy and states are clear.
- Treat uncertainty as first-class: missing snapshots, failed syncs, stale data, and abnormal values need explicit UI states.
- Keep MF_EP read-only: the data center observes, snapshots, governs, and serves metrics without changing business workflows.
- Design for repeated daily use: fast scanning, stable navigation, predictable controls, and restrained motion matter more than surprise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline. Maintain readable contrast for body text and controls, visible focus states, keyboard-operable forms and tables, reduced-motion-safe transitions, and color-independent status labels for success, warning, error, stale, and missing data.
